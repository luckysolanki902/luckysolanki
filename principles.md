# Research-Backed UI Design Principles  
*(Aligned with your 2026 Portfolio philosophy)*

---

## 1. Hick’s Law  
**Fewer choices = faster decisions.**

- 3 nav links  
- 3–4 projects  
- No skill logo grid  
- No unnecessary form fields  

Reduce options until clarity increases without removing personality.

---

## 2. Fitts’s Law  
**Bigger + closer targets are easier to click.**

- Minimum 44×44px tap targets  
- Primary CTA slightly larger click area  
- Important actions near reading flow  

---

## 3. Gestalt Principles

### Proximity  
Related items are close. Unrelated items are far.

### Similarity  
Interactive elements should look consistent.

### Hierarchy  
Size + weight + spacing create structure before color does.

---

## 4. Von Restorff Effect  
**If everything stands out, nothing stands out.**

Neutral UI.  
Color comes from the work.

---

## 5. Cognitive Load Theory  
Working memory holds ~4 chunks at once.

Apply:
- No tech stack dump in hero  
- Progressive disclosure  
- Max 65ch line length  
- Density increases as scroll deepens  

---

## 6. Progressive Disclosure  
Surface = calm  
Depth = rich  

---

## 7. Peak-End Rule  
Users remember:
- The first 5 seconds  
- The final emotional note  

Consider ending with:
> “Let’s build something people actually use.”

---

## 8. White Space Increases Perceived Value  

Whitespace signals confidence and clarity.

When in doubt:
Add 10% more space.

---

## 9. Visual Hierarchy & Eye Scanning  

Users scan:
- F-pattern (text-heavy areas)  
- Z-pattern (landing sections)

Structure:
- Claim → proof → depth  
- Screenshot → title → context → stack  

---

## 10. System Consistency  

Strong systems create trust:
- Defined spacing scale  
- Defined type scale  
- Defined motion system  
- Limited color use  

Consistency reduces cognitive friction.

---

# Strategic Refinement Suggestions

## 1. Reduce to Two Fonts  

Stronger system:
- Quicksand (display)
- Inter (everything else)


---

## 2. Tighten the Hero Line  

Current:
> I build products that people actually use.

Sharper:
> I build products people actually use.

---

## 3. Add One Metric in Hero  

Instead of:
> Building things since 2021.

Try:
> Built products used by 50K+ monthly users.

Specificity builds credibility.

---

## 4. Rename “Capabilities”  

Instead of:
> Capabilities  

Consider:
> What I build with  

More maker. Less résumé.

---

## 5. Consider Removing Theme Toggle  

Premium portfolios often:
- Choose one mode  
- Commit fully  

Ask:
Does it increase conversion?  
If not — remove it.
